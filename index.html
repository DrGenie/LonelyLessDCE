LonelyLessAustralia Decision Aid Tool

Loneliness affects over five million Australians, especially older adults, resulting in significant health and economic burdens. This online decision aid tool is developed from a discrete choice experiment that identified older adults’ preferences for programmes aimed at reducing loneliness in Australia. Utilising these DCE insights and estimates, the tool predicts uptake probabilities for different programme packages, enabling personalised services based on key factors older adults consider when seeking support.

The loneliness categories used in this study were derived from the widely validated De Jong Gierveld Loneliness Scale (11-item version). This scale measures both emotional and social loneliness, and a total loneliness score was calculated as their sum. Following the established methodology, these scores were classified into three levels—Not Lonely, Moderately Lonely, and Severely Lonely—facilitating meaningful comparisons of preferences across different levels of loneliness severity.

Programme Attributes

Cost: The out-of-pocket cost participants incur each time they join a session.

Accessibility: The ease or difficulty of attending, considering travel distance and mobility.

Frequency of Interaction: How often individuals participate (e.g., weekly, monthly, or daily reference).

Method of Interaction: In-person, virtual-only, or hybrid; crucial for those with limited mobility or a preference for digital connection.

Duration of Each Interaction: The length of each session (e.g., 30 min, 2 hours, 4 hours).

Type of Support Programme: The nature of support: peer support, community engagement, psychological counselling, virtual reality, etc.

Some costs may be adjusted by Cost-of-Living multipliers depending on the chosen state. Proceed to Inputs to explore scenarios.

Cost Components & Benefits

Costs may include:

  • Staff/Personnel
  • Facilities & Equipment
  • Marketing & Outreach
  • Training & Certification
  • Miscellaneous Operational Expenses
  • Cost-of-Living Multipliers (by state)

Benefits are modelled by attributing a monetary value per 1% improvement in uptake. These figures can be adjusted to reflect real-world data and validated cost-benefit evaluations.
