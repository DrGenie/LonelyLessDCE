Attribute definitions

The tool mirrors the attributes and levels used in the discrete choice experiment with older adults.

Attribute Description Levels (as used in the DCE)
Type of support program How adults connect with others and what kinds of activities they might do together. Peer Support, Community Engagement, Psychological Counselling, Virtual Reality (VR)
Method of engagement or interaction How individuals connect, including options that suit people with mobility limitations or a preference for digital communication. In person, Virtual, Hybrid (virtual and in person)
Frequency of interaction or engagement The regularity of participation in the program. Daily, Weekly, Monthly
Duration of each interaction The length of each interaction or activity. 30 minutes, 2 hours, 4 hours
Accessibility or convenience The ease of accessing the program, considering physical mobility and transport. At home, Local area (up to 12 km travel), Wider community travel (50+ km)
Costs of support program The out of pocket cost older adults incur each time they participate in the support program. Free, AUD 5 per session, AUD 20 per session, AUD 50 per session

In the decision aid, these attributes are translated into continuous uptake and cost projections. Peer Support, daily participation, 30 minute sessions, at home delivery and free cost form the reference categories in the underlying model.
