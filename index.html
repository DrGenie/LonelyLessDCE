LonelyLessAustralia Decision Aid Tool

Loneliness affects over five million Australians, especially older adults, leading to significant health and economic challenges. This tool draws on a discrete choice experiment (DCE) to forecast programme uptake probabilities, informing personalised services grounded in older adults’ priorities.

Navigate with the tabs above:

  • Instructions – Guidance for each tool feature.
  • Attributes – A detailed description of each DCE attribute.
  • Inputs – Configure your scenario’s cost, accessibility, etc.
  • WTP Results – A bar chart of willingness-to-pay (A$) for main model attributes, with possible p-values.
  • Program Uptake Probability – Dynamically computed bar chart based on your current selections.
  • Costs & Benefits – Outlines cost-of-living and the A\$5,000 per 1% improvement assumption.
  • Scenarios – Save your scenarios to compare or export PDF reports with advanced charts.

How to Use This Tool

1. Check the Attributes tab to learn about each dimension in the DCE.

2. On the Inputs tab, choose your state (optional), cost-of-living (yes/no), cost per session, accessibility (local/wider), frequency (weekly/monthly), method (virtual/hybrid), duration (2H/4H), and support type (community engagement, counselling, VR).

3. Click “Calculate & View Results” to open a separate window with cost-benefit charts and predicted programme uptake. You can also “Show Probability Plot” on the “Program Uptake Probability” tab to see how these attributes affect uptake.

4. The WTP Results tab displays a bar chart for the main model, possibly including confidence intervals and significance where relevant.

5. Under Scenarios, save or compare multiple configurations side by side, generating PDF exports if desired.

Programme Attributes

Cost per session: The fee (AUD) participants pay out-of-pocket each time.

Accessibility: Whether the programme is local (up to 12 km) or in a wider community (50+ km), impacting mobility, transport time, and possible travel barriers.

Frequency of Interaction: Daily is the reference scenario in the DCE; weekly and monthly are alternates, influencing how often participants engage socially.

Method of Interaction: In-person, fully virtual, or hybrid (both in-person and online), to cater for mobility, location preferences, and user tech comfort.

Duration of Each Interaction: Ranging from brief 30-min sessions (reference) to 2-hour or 4-hour extended engagements, which can affect depth of connection and fatigue.

Type of Support Programme: Peer support (reference), community engagement, counselling, or VR experiences, each offering different levels of social contact and potential benefits.

Cost Components & Benefits

A A\$5,000 benefit for each 1% improvement in programme uptake is assumed, alongside cost-of-living multipliers if the user selects “yes.” Potential costs include:

  • Personnel (staff salaries, volunteers, etc.)
  • Facilities & Equipment (rent, IT services)
  • Marketing & Outreach (advertising, community events)
  • Training & Certification (workshops, materials)
  • Miscellaneous Operational Expenses (transport, admin, refreshments)
